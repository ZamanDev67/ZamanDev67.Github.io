NIMA SHARIAT ZAMANPOUR

ABOUT


Nima Shariat Zamanpour is an architect, artist and educator based in New York City. He currently serves as a project manager at the Metropolitan Museum of Art and is the co-founder of TULPA, a research & consulting group that explores the connections between architecture, language, craft, and technology. Nima earned his Master of Architecture from the Harvard Graduate School of Design and holds Bachelors' degrees in Computer Science, Economics, and Art from UC Santa Cruz. He has taught at Yale, RISD, the BAC, and Wentworth, and has guest lectured at Harvard and UWM.

Nima's research interests encompass computational graphics, AI, urban planning, survivalism, simulations, the occult and Science and Technology Studies (STS).
